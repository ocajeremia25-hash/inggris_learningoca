Grammar Material

Simple Present Tense

Formula: Subject + Verb 1 (+s/es)

Usage: Daily habits, general facts, routines

Examples:

  • She plays football every day.
  • He works in a office.
  • They go to school on weekdays.

Simple Past Tense

Formula: Subject + Verb 2 (ed) or Irregular Verbs

Usage: Completed actions in the past

Examples:

  • She played football yesterday.
  • He worked hard last week.
  • They went to the beach last summer.

Present Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing

Usage: Actions happening right now

Examples:

  • She is playing football right now.
  • He is working on a project.
  • They are watching a movie.

Simple Future Tense

Formula: Subject + will + Verb 1

Usage: Future predictions, plans, promises

Examples:

  • She will play football tomorrow.
  • He will go to university next year.
  • They will meet us at the station.

Present Perfect Tense

Formula: Subject + have/has + Verb 3 (past participle)

Usage: Recent past experiences, life experiences

Examples:

  • She has played football before.
  • He has lived here for 5 years.
  • They have visited Paris three times.

Articles (A, An, The)

A: Used before consonant sounds

Example: a cat, a book, a university

An: Used before vowel sounds

Example: an apple, an elephant, an hour

The: Used for specific things

Example: the book, the teacher, the sun

Personal Pronouns

Subject Pronouns: I, You, He, She, It, We, They

Example: I am happy, She is intelligent

Object Pronouns: me, you, him, her, it, us, them

Example: He loves me, Give it to her

Common Prepositions

Time: at, in, on, during, after, before

Example: at 3 o'clock, in the morning, on Monday

Place: in, on, at, under, above, between, near

Example: in the house, on the table, at school

Question Formation (Wh- Questions)

Who: About people

Example: Who is your teacher?

What: About things/actions

Example: What is your name?

Where: About places

Example: Where do you live?

When: About time

Example: When is your birthday?
